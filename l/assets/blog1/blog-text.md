### STEP 3 — How to Choose the Right Size for Your Quilt Coat Sewing Pattern

Every body is different, so choosing the correct size is crucial for a great fit. Using a measuring tape, gently measure your bust, waist, and hips. Keep the tape comfortably fitted — not too tight or too loose — to ensure accuracy.

Compare your measurements with the pattern's size chart. The Siena Quilt Coat sewing pattern covers sizes XXS through 5XL, making it one of the most inclusive women's clothing patterns available. If your measurements fall between two sizes, we recommend choosing the larger one for a more relaxed and comfortable fit, which is ideal for a layering piece like a quilted jacket.

### STEP 4 — How to Sew a Welt Pocket on a Quilted Jacket

One of the most intimidating parts of jacket sewing is the pockets, but this is where a well-designed sewing pattern makes all the difference. Our pocket method guarantees a clean, professional finish without the bulk of a patch pocket.

After applying a small piece of interfacing to the wrong side of the jacket front for stability, stitch your folded pocket piece right sides together with the jacket, aligning it perfectly with your marked rectangle.

The crucial step is cutting straight down the center of the stitched rectangle, stopping exactly 1 cm before each end, and snipping diagonally into the corners to form small triangles. Pulling the pocket pieces through that opening to the wrong side of the jacket creates a perfectly crisp, rectangular pocket opening.

### STEP 5 — How to Sew Set-In Sleeves on a Quilt Coat

With the pockets secure, we move on to the main structure. Place the front and back pieces right sides together, pinning along the shoulder and side seams.

For the sleeves, gently pull the gathering threads to ease the sleeve cap into the armhole, adjusting until it matches perfectly before pinning and sewing. A great jacket needs balanced proportions, so we designed a sleek, fitted wrist rather than a wide sleeve, providing a structured, modern silhouette that keeps the cold out.

### STEP 6 — How to Sew the Hood on a Quilt Coat

The hood is one of the most satisfying parts of the Siena coat — it gives the garment its signature silhouette. You will sew the outer hood and the lining hood separately before joining them together.

Start with the outer hood pieces: pin them right sides together along the curved center seam, aligning the edges carefully. Using a 1 cm seam allowance, stitch along the curved seam following the stitching line on your pattern. Repeat the exact same process for the lining hood pieces.

Once both the outer hood and lining hood are sewn, place them right sides together, align the front edges, and pin along the face opening. Stitch along the opening edge, trim the seam allowance, and turn right side out. Press the edge flat for a crisp, clean finish before attaching it to the jacket body.

### STEP 7 — How to Fully Line a Jacket (The Sandwich Method)

A fully lined interior is what separates a truly polished fashion pattern from a basic sewing project. It elevates your quilted sewing to a professional level and is a hallmark of quality women's jacket sewing. To achieve this without raw seams showing, we use a specific bagging-out technique.

Place your assembled main jacket flat with the hood positioned downward and the sleeves folded inward. Place your fully assembled lining directly on top, right sides together, ensuring the sleeves are safely tucked inside like a sandwich.

Once stitched around the perimeter, you will pull the entire jacket right-side out through the bottom hem and press the edges for a crisp, seamless finish.

### STEP 8 — How to Finish Hems with Bias Binding on a Quilt Coat

For the absolute highest quality finish on your quilted sewing project, we apply bias binding to the wrist hems and the bottom hem. This detail is what makes the Siena stand apart from other women's clothing patterns — it is the finishing touch that gives the garment a truly couture feel.

Unfold one edge of your prepared bias tape, align it with the raw edge of your jacket hem (right sides together), and stitch. Fold the tape over to the inside to completely encase the raw seam, and topstitch it down close to the folded edge. This provides a durable, beautifully handcrafted touch that ensures your jacket lasts for years.
