Printing

Use A4 or US Letter paper and open the file in Adobe Acrobat Reader (free to download). In the print settings, select Actual Size — or Do Not Scale — and make sure all automatic fit, resize, and margin adjustment options are turned off.

The A4 and US Letter files are both sized to print correctly on either paper size, so you do not need to adjust anything beyond setting the scale to 100%.

Each pattern includes a test square on the first page. Print that page first and measure the square — if it matches the dimensions printed on it, everything is set correctly before you print the rest.

Avoid printing from your browser's built-in PDF viewer. It can strip layers and alter page scaling, which shifts your pattern pieces.

The A0 file is a single large-format sheet that contains all the pattern pieces without any page breaks or tape lines. Take it to a copy shop or print bureau that offers wide-format or plotter printing and ask for an A0 print at 100% scale.

This saves a significant amount of time compared to taping together an A4 or US Letter version, and the result is a clean single sheet you can use directly on your fabric.

Yes. Every LU2COHOUSE pattern includes seam allowance — you do not need to add your own. Most patterns use a 1 cm seam allowance throughout. The exact amount is specified in the product description and printed at the beginning of the pattern booklet.

Every LU2COHOUSE pattern includes all sizes from XXS to 5XL in a single purchase — you receive the full size range and can cut the size that fits you, or blend between sizes for a closer fit.

To choose your size, check the size chart in the listing photos for the specific pattern you are buying. Compare your body measurements — bust, waist, and hip — directly against the chart. Do not go by your ready-to-wear clothing size, as garment sizing varies significantly across brands.

If your measurements fall between two sizes, we generally recommend sizing up. You can always take in a seam, but adding ease back in requires more work.
